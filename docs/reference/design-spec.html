NEXA OPS HANDBOOK

NEXA's internal operations platform — the single destination for every process, credential, document, tool, and piece of team knowledge across the agency
Internal Platform Vision — April 2026
One place for everything
SOPs, platform credentials, team contacts, internal communications, documents, and agency tools — all in one authenticated platform. No more searching Slack for a link, digging through Drive for a procedure, or asking a colleague for a login. Everything operational lives here.
One person keeps it accurate
The Operations Manager owns the Handbook. All content decisions, updates, and structural changes go through them. Departments can feed data into designated sections through processes the Operations Manager defines — but the integrity and accuracy of the platform rests with one accountable person.
A launchpad, not just a reference
The Handbook is also where staff access every tool NEXA has built — the Pricing Calculator, the Sales Portal, and any future internal platforms. One login. One place. Every resource the team needs to do their job well.
01 — What the platform is
Platform type
Authenticated Internal Web Application
The NEXA Ops Handbook is a full web application accessible at a dedicated NEXA domain. Staff log in once using their NEXA credentials via Single Sign-On — the same login used across all NEXA tools. The platform is entirely internal. Every section, every record, and every tool link is built for the NEXA team only. Nothing is externally visible.
Access
Full Access for All Staff
Once logged in, every NEXA team member has access to the full platform. There are no restricted sections or role-gated content. The Handbook is a shared resource for the entire agency — the assumption is that everyone on the team benefits from knowing how the business operates. Transparency is a feature, not a risk.
Ownership
Operations Manager as Content Controller
All content within the Handbook is owned and controlled by the Operations Manager. They decide what is published, when it is updated, and how it is structured. Departments can contribute information through defined submission processes — but the Operations Manager is the gatekeeper. Nothing goes live without their approval.
Departments submit → Operations Manager reviews and publishes.
The Operations Manager can set up lightweight external processes — a shared form, a recurring email, a Notion page — that allow individual departments to prepare and submit their own updates. This keeps content distributed in its creation but centralised in its publication. The Handbook stays accurate because one person is responsible for it.
02 — Login and the Home dashboard
Authentication
NEXA SSO — One Login, Everything Unlocked
Staff log in using their NEXA credentials via Single Sign-On. The Handbook connects to NEXA's central identity provider — the same system managing access to Google Workspace, Slack, and all agency tools. A new team member added to NEXA's system gains immediate Handbook access. A team member who leaves has their access revoked the moment their NEXA account is deactivated. No separate password, no manual user management on the Handbook side.
Navigation
Persistent Sidebar — Always in View
After login, staff land on the Home dashboard. A fixed left sidebar shows the NEXA brand mark, the platform name, and all navigation sections. The sidebar is always visible regardless of which section is open — the active section is highlighted. Staff can move between any section instantly. The Home dashboard also shows the live news ticker and a summary of what is new or recently updated across the platform.

04 — SOPs
The agency's full procedure library — searchable, organised, and always current
Owned by the Operations Manager
SOPs in the Handbook are the single authoritative reference for how NEXA does things. Each procedure is written and published by the Operations Manager, who coordinates with department heads to ensure accuracy. When a process changes, the Operations Manager updates the relevant SOP and the change is immediately visible to the whole team. There is no version confusion, no outdated PDF, and no "which one is the latest?" — there is only the Handbook.
What each SOP contains
Procedure name, owning department, the step-by-step process, any important notes or exceptions, last updated date, and linked templates or external resources the staff member needs to execute the procedure.
Organised by department
SOPs are tagged by department — Social, Content, Design, Web, Strategy, Finance, Operations. Staff can filter by their department to see only what is relevant to them, or browse the full library. Full-text search works across all SOPs.
How departments contribute
Department heads submit SOP drafts or updates to the Operations Manager through a defined process — a shared form, a structured email, or a dedicated submission template. The Operations Manager reviews, edits, and publishes. The department has input; the Handbook has one owner.
Linked resources
Each SOP links directly to any templates, forms, or tools the staff member needs. Opening an SOP gives you everything required to follow it — no hunting for the right file in a shared drive.
05 — Platform logins and credentials
Every tool NEXA uses — access instructions and credentials in one place
Updated centrally by the Operations Manager
The Platform Logins section replaces scattered spreadsheets, Slack messages hunting for passwords, and "ask the AM" approaches to tool access. Every platform NEXA uses — social scheduling, analytics, design tools, reporting systems, AI tools, project management, finance platforms — is listed here with everything a staff member needs to access and use it. When credentials change, the Operations Manager updates the Handbook once and the entire team sees the correct information immediately.
What's listed per tool
Tool name and category, login method (SSO, shared account, or individual), access instructions, account tier and plan details, and the name of the person who owns or manages the account at NEXA.
How credentials are displayed
Shared credentials are shown directly on the tool's card. Tools that require individual accounts provide step-by-step instructions for requesting access. Sensitive credentials include a brief note on responsible use alongside the login details.
Categories
Social and scheduling, analytics and reporting, creative and design, AI and productivity, project management, finance and billing, communication tools. Every platform organised by how it is used, not alphabetically.
When a platform is added, changed, or discontinued, the Operations Manager updates this section. Staff are notified via the news ticker. The Handbook is always the current reference — if a credential shown here does not work, that is a signal to report to the Operations Manager that an update is needed.
06 — Team directory
Directory
Every NEXA Team Member, in One View
The Team section shows every NEXA staff member — their department, role, and direct contact options. Staff are displayed as cards in a filterable grid. Filter by department to see Social, Content, Design, Web, Strategy, Finance, or Operations. All profiles are created and maintained by the Admin. Staff do not edit their own profiles.
Contact
One-Click to Reach Anyone
Each profile card shows the staff member's name, department, role, and direct contact buttons — email, Slack handle, and WhatsApp where applicable. One click opens the relevant communication. No searching for email addresses or Slack handles. The Handbook is the agency phonebook.
Structure
Org Chart View
Alongside the card grid, a second view shows the full NEXA organisational chart — who reports to whom, how departments are structured, and where each role sits in the agency hierarchy. Useful for new starters orienting themselves, and for leadership reviewing team structure at a glance.
All team profiles are managed by the Admin. When a new team member joins, the Admin creates their profile. When someone leaves or changes role, the Admin updates it. The directory is always a reflection of the current team — not who was here six months ago.

09 — Internal communications
How NEXA communicates internally — standards, templates, and an archive of what has been said
The Internal Comms section documents how NEXA's team communicates with each other and across departments. It covers which channels are used for what, how quickly staff are expected to respond, how to escalate, and how meetings should be run. Email and message templates for common scenarios are available here so staff can communicate consistently without drafting from scratch. Company announcements published via the news ticker are archived in this section so every piece of important communication is searchable and permanent.
Templates library
Pre-written templates for internal requests, escalations, meeting agendas, feedback, and handovers. Staff copy and adapt them. Templates reflect NEXA's communication standards — professional, clear, and direct. Published and maintained by the Operations Manager.
Announcements archive
Every company-wide announcement that has appeared in the news ticker is archived here — searchable, dated, and permanent. A new team member can read the full history of what has been communicated across the business since the Handbook launched.
Channels
What Goes Where
Which conversations belong in Slack, which in email, and which warrant a meeting — documented and agreed. Expected response times per channel. When to use a direct message versus a channel post. Clear standards that prevent over-communication in some places and under-communication in others.
Escalation
Escalation Paths
For every type of internal issue — a project blocking, a resource gap, a client complaint, an interpersonal conflict — the escalation path is documented. Who to go to first, what context to bring, and what to expect back. Staff never feel stuck not knowing how to raise something important.
Meetings
Meeting Standards
When to book a meeting versus send a message. How to write an agenda. How minutes should be recorded and distributed. How to follow up on action items. Meeting culture is part of how NEXA operates — these are the standards that keep meetings productive and rare.
10 — Onboarding for new employees
New starters have one place to begin — a structured checklist they work through on arrival
When a new team member joins NEXA, their first stop is the Onboarding section of the Handbook. It presents a structured checklist of everything they need to read, set up, and confirm in their first week. Each item links to the relevant section of the Handbook or an external resource. As they complete each step, they mark it as done — giving them a clear view of their progress and giving the Operations Manager confirmation that the joining process has been completed properly.

Suggestions for future updates
Ideas worth building when the platform is established and the team is using it well.
SOCIALVERSE Integration
SOCIALVERSE is NEXA's social media operating system — a separate internal platform. As both mature, the Handbook's SOP and document library could surface SOCIALVERSE-specific procedures in context. An AM working in SOCIALVERSE could access the relevant SOP without switching to the Handbook manually. A link between the two platforms makes both more useful.
Suggested when both platforms are fully adopted by the team.
Department Dashboards
A lightweight "My Department" view personalised to the logged-in user's team — showing only their department's SOPs, relevant tools, their team card, and their department's most recently updated documents. Not a restriction, but a convenience layer that makes the platform faster to use for day-to-day work without navigating the full library every time.
Suggested after the platform has at least 3 months of usage data.
SOP Submission Portal
A structured built-in form that department heads use to submit new or updated SOPs directly to the Operations Manager. Currently this happens via external tools (a shared form or email). Bringing the submission process into the Handbook itself would close the loop — everything to do with SOPs, from drafting to publication, lives in one place.
Suggested once the SOP library has reached full coverage across all departments.
Staff Recognition Program
A peer-recognition system using the three NEXA coins — Sage (wise counsel), Arbiter (resolves challenges), Champion (exceeds expectations). Staff nominate colleagues, managers confirm awards, and recognition is published to a feed and pushed to Slack as custom emoji. Tracks recognition history per person over time. A culture feature worth building once the operational foundation is solid.
Suggested for Phase 2 — when the platform is stable and actively used.
Google Workspace Deep Integration
Currently the Documents section links to files in Google Drive. A deeper integration would allow documents to be previewed within the Handbook without leaving the platform — and could surface recently accessed or frequently used Drive documents per user on their Home dashboard, based on their Google Workspace activity.
Suggested when a dedicated technical resource is available to build the integration.
Handbook Usage Analytics
A simple admin view showing which sections are most visited, which SOPs are most accessed, and which tools are clicked most often from the Tools section. Gives the Operations Manager data to prioritise what to improve and evidence that the platform is being used. Helps identify gaps — sections that are rarely visited may indicate missing content or unclear navigation.
Suggested after 6 months of operation — when there is meaningful data to analyse.
